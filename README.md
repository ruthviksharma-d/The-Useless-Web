## What Is This

You click a button. You get teleported to a random corner of the internet.

It might be a horse with infinitely long legs. It might be someone slapping a stranger with an eel. It might be a website that plays a tiny tuba at you for no reason. It might be a genie that correctly guesses every fictional character you've ever thought of.

That's it. That's the product.

No sign-up. No onboarding flow. No "we noticed you haven't completed your profile." No cookie banners that take up half the screen. No recommendation algorithm trained on your deepest fears. Just pure, uncut internet chaos вҖ” served cold, directly into your face.

---

## The Backstory

This project started because someone asked *"whatever happened to the fun part of the internet?"*

There was a time when people built websites at 2 AM just because they had an idea. No monetization strategy. No growth metrics. No product-market fit. Just: *"what if a horse's legs just... kept going?"* And then they built it. And then the internet loved it.

That era never died. It just got buried under SEO-optimized listicles and VC-funded productivity apps.

Useless Universe digs it back up.

## Achievements

There are 12 achievement badges hidden inside the app. You unlock them by exploring.

We're not listing them here. Half the fun is finding out what triggers them.

What we will say:
- At least one requires visiting something genuinely disturbing
- At least one requires patience that most people don't have
- The rarest one has been unlocked by very few people in testing
- They persist in `localStorage`, so they survive page refreshes but not private browsing

*(The achievement system was built before anyone asked whether a random internet site explorer needed an achievement system. It does. Obviously it does.)*

## FAQ

**Q: Is this actually useless?**
A: Completely. That's the entire point.

**Q: Who is this for?**
A: People who remember when stumbling onto a weird website was exciting. People who are bored. People who are supposed to be in a meeting right now.

**Q: Why does it track achievements?**
A: Because exploring weird websites should feel like an adventure, not a chore. Also because we could.

**Q: Can I submit a website?**
A: Yes. Open an issue. It must be weird, harmless, and ideally inexplicable.

**Q: Is my data sold to advertisers?**
A: There is no server. There is no database. There is no data. There is only `localStorage` and chaos.

**Q: What's the weirdest site in here?**
A: Zombo.com. Always Zombo.com. It has been the correct answer to this question since 1999.

## Contributing

Found a site that belongs in the universe? It must pass the admission criteria:

- [ ] It serves no practical purpose whatsoever
- [ ] You instinctively want to show it to someone immediately
- [ ] You cannot fully explain why it exists
- [ ] You could describe it in one sentence and it would still sound made up
- [ ] It is not harmful, illegal, or NSFW

Open an issue with the URL and a one-line description. If it's weird enough, it gets in. If it's *too* weird, it goes on the rejected list above and becomes lore.

Code contributions are welcome too. Just keep it weird.

## Philosophy

The modern internet is optimized. Personalized. Monetized. Every pixel is A/B tested against a control group. Every scroll is a data point. Every recommendation is a calculation designed to maximize time-on-site and minimize cognitive discomfort.

The result is an internet that shows you exactly what you want, and nothing you didn't expect.

This is the opposite of that.

Useless Universe is a monument to the idea that the internet is more interesting when it surprises you. When something exists not because a product manager approved it, but because someone thought it would be funny. When discovery feels like wandering, not navigation.

The sites in here weren't built to scale. They weren't built to convert. They were built because the internet once had this beautiful quality вҖ” that absolutely anyone could put absolutely anything on it, and absolutely anyone else could stumble into it at 11pm on a Tuesday and think *"what is this and why does it exist and why can I not stop looking at it."*

We're trying to protect that.
